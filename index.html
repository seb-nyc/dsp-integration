Risk Management Framework

Risk identification and mitigation strategies across all impact levels

Operational challenges that may extend timelines or create knowledge gaps, but have manageable business impact with proper planning.

Technical Discovery Issues

Risk:

Unexpected technical requirements can extend the integration timeline beyond the 2-month target

Mitigation:

Comprehensive integration questionnaire and thorough technical validation during scoping phase

Knowledge Gaps

Risk:

Integration is live but capabilities are not fully documented, leaving teams unsure how to push deals or advise on setup/activation

Mitigation:

Robust documentation process, training materials creation, and knowledge transfer sessions with all stakeholder teams

Operational risks that could delay timelines or impact campaign performance, requiring proactive management and clear mitigation plans.

Engineering Resource Delays

Risk:

Code changes compete with other priorities, potentially missing campaign launch deadlines and revenue targets

Mitigation:

Early engineering alignment and clear business impact communication to prioritize resources

Testing Scope Gaps

Risk:

Missing edge cases can cause activation issues with high-value campaigns

Mitigation:

Comprehensive scoping phase and detailed test plans based on DSP capabilities assessment

Contract/Legal Delays

Risk:

Incomplete paperwork prevents go-live, impacting revenue pipeline and client expectations

Mitigation:

Front-load contract validation and credit checks in pre-check phase

Commercial Handoff Gaps

Risk:

Live integration with no clear account plan or commercial relationships prevents revenue generation

Mitigation:

Establish clear commercial handoff process with account management and business development teams

Critical risks that could significantly impact business operations and revenue, requiring immediate attention and robust mitigation strategies.

Billing Discrepancies

Risk:

Significant financial losses when publishers expect full payment but DSP undercounts spend

Mitigation:

Establish clear source of truth, implement automated daily reconciliation, require minimal spend discrepancies during testing phase

DSP Infrastructure Impact

Risk:

DSP returning excessive bids or misconfigured requests can degrade DSP platform performance, affecting all clients

Mitigation:

Gradual QPS scaling with real-time monitoring, teams on standby for immediate rollback procedures

DSP Endpoint Overwhelm

Risk:

Scaling too quickly can crash DSP bidder, impacting their campaigns across all SSPs

Mitigation:

Controlled traffic increase with comprehensive DSP performance monitoring and communication

Lessons from Experience

Key insights and best practices from previous DSP integrations

Monitoring Excellence

Good monitoring is essential for early issue detection and resolution. Success requires actionable messaging, proper team training, and thorough documentation. Implementing DSP tier systems reduces noise and improves response times.

Commercial Context Matters

Integration approach must align with commercial strategy. Self-service SMB buyers have different needs and expectations compared to large HoldCo clients. Educating clients on programmatic possibilities can be challenging but is essential for success.

Documentation Drives Efficiency

Documenting pitfalls and edge cases significantly improves future integrations. Sharing detailed checklists with DSPs highlighting common issues has proven to accelerate development timelines and reduce troubleshooting cycles.

Integration Timing Strategy

Rolling additional API integrations into the main integration phase works better than bolt-on approaches later. DSPs are more engaged and have dedicated resources during integration, making it the optimal time for complete setup.

Collaborative Problem-Solving

Asking for help early in the process is crucial - team members may have encountered similar issues before. Using collective experience accelerates resolution and prevents repeated mistakes across integrations.

Automation Opportunities

Many tasks repeat across integrations, creating opportunities for templating and automation. Standardizing setup processes, deal creation, traffic management, and reconciliation with DSPs improves efficiency and reduces manual errors.
